# References & Further Reading

## Primary Framework Author

### Teresa Torres — Continuous Discovery Habits

Teresa Torres developed the 6-Layer Problem Definition Framework as part of her continuous discovery practice. Her work bridges lean product development, opportunity solution trees, and structured customer research.

- **Book**: [*Continuous Discovery Habits*](https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/) — Product Talk, 2021. The foundational text. Chapters on opportunity framing and problem definition are most directly relevant to this skill.
- **Website**: [continuousdiscoveryrhabits.com](https://www.continuousdiscoveryhabits.com/) — course, templates, and community
- **Blog**: [producttalk.org](https://www.producttalk.org/) — ongoing writing on discovery practice, opportunity solution trees, assumption testing
- **Podcast**: [Continuous Discovery Podcast](https://www.producttalk.org/category/podcast/) — interviews with practitioners applying the framework
- **LinkedIn**: [Teresa Torres](https://www.linkedin.com/in/teresatorres/) — active with case studies and nuance on the framework

**Key concept to understand before running the framework**: Torres distinguishes between *outcomes* (what the business wants to achieve), *opportunities* (customer needs, pain points, and desires), and *solutions* (what the team builds). The 6-Layer framework lives entirely in the opportunity space — it is how you deeply understand the problem before you consider solutions.

## Behavioral Science Frameworks (Layer 3)

### BJ Fogg — Behavior Model (B=MAP)

Fogg's model states that behavior occurs when Motivation, Ability, and Prompt converge simultaneously. Central to understanding why users adopt (or resist) new tools.

- **Model site**: [behaviormodel.org](https://www.behaviormodel.org/)
- **Book**: [*Tiny Habits*](https://www.tinyhabits.com/) — BJ Fogg, 2020. Accessible application of B=MAP to habit formation.
- **Lab**: [Behavior Design Lab at Stanford](https://behaviordesign.stanford.edu/)

**Application in the framework**: Use B=MAP in Layer 3 to diagnose which component is missing — is the user *unmotivated*, *unable*, or simply *never prompted*? Different diagnoses lead to different interventions.

---

### Fred Davis — Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

TAM identifies Perceived Usefulness and Perceived Ease of Use as the two primary determinants of technology adoption intention.

- **Original paper**: Davis, F.D. (1989). "Perceived Usefulness, Perceived Ease of Use, and User Acceptance of Information Technology." *MIS Quarterly*, 13(3), 319–340. [doi:10.2307/249008](https://doi.org/10.2307/249008)
- **Overview**: [TAM — ScienceDirect Topics](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/social-sciences/technology-acceptance-model)
- **Developer tools application**: [Explaining Software Development Tool Use with TAM](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/08874417.2001.11647015)

**Application in the framework**: In Layer 3, TAM helps frame whether behavioral blockers are primarily about *perceived value* (does the user believe this will help them?) or *friction* (does the user find it too hard to try?). These map to different product interventions.

---

### Everett Rogers — Diffusion of Innovations

Rogers' five adoption attributes — Relative Advantage, Compatibility, Complexity, Trialability, Observability — predict how quickly an innovation spreads through a population.

- **Book**: [*Diffusion of Innovations*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion_of_innovations), 5th ed., 2003. Free University Press Digital Library has excerpts.
- **Wikipedia overview**: [Diffusion of Innovations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion_of_innovations) — useful for the five attributes table
- **Original adoption curve**: Rogers coined "innovators / early adopters / early majority / late majority / laggards" — the S-curve still applies directly to Layer 3 analysis.

**Application in the framework**: Use Rogers in Layer 3 to assess where your product sits on the adoption curve and which attribute is the primary blocker. Complexity (too hard to try) vs. Observability (no visible results) require very different interventions.

## Cognitive Load & Developer Behavior (Layer 3)

### Cognitive Load Theory

- **Sweller, J.** (1988). "Cognitive Load During Problem Solving." *Cognitive Science*, 12(2), 257–285. Foundational paper.
- **Practical overview**: [Cognitive Load Theory for UX](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/minimize-cognitive-load/) — Nielsen Norman Group
- **Feature discovery application**: Microsoft internal study (2006) finding 90% of requested features already existed — users simply didn't know. See [Demystifying AI Coding Assistants](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.12285v1) for modern replication.

---

### Context Switching Research

- **Gloria Mark** — UC Irvine research on interruption recovery. Summary: [It Takes 23 Minutes to Regain Focus](https://www.fastcompany.com/944128/worker-interrupted-cost-task-switching) — Fast Company
- **METR Study (2025)**: Measured developer task performance with and without AI tools; found developers were 19% *slower* with AI assistance yet predicted they'd be 24% faster. [METR research](https://metr.org/)

## Product Discovery & Ecosystem Research (Layers 1–2)

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

Complements Torres' framework — useful for framing Layer 1 customer mental models.

- **Clayton Christensen** — original JTBD framing: [*The Innovator's Dilemma*](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=46)
- **Bob Moesta & Chris Spiek** — [Re-Wired Group](https://therewiredgroup.com/), practitioners who operationalized JTBD interviews
- **Alan Klement** — [*When Coffee and Kale Compete*](http://www.whencoffeeandkalecompete.com/) — free online, practical JTBD application

---

### Opportunity Solution Trees

Torres' own method for organizing discovery findings into actionable structure.

- **Introduction**: [Opportunity Solution Trees — producttalk.org](https://www.producttalk.org/2023/12/opportunity-solution-trees/)
- **Template**: Available in the [Continuous Discovery Habits course](https://www.continuousdiscoveryhabits.com/) and on Miro

## Recommended Reading Order

For someone new to the framework:

1. Torres — *Continuous Discovery Habits* (Chapters 1–5 for the opportunity framing)
2. Fogg — *Tiny Habits* (Chapters 1–3 for B=MAP intuition)
3. Rogers — Diffusion of Innovations Wikipedia article (5 attributes only)
4. Nielsen Norman Group — Cognitive Load article
5. Torres — Opportunity Solution Trees blog post (to connect research output to what comes next)
